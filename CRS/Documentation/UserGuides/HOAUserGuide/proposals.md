# Proposals

When your reserve study request is approved, you'll receive a proposal outlining the scope of work, pricing, and terms. This guide explains how to review and accept proposals.

---

## Understanding Proposals

A proposal is a formal service agreement between your community and the reserve study company. It includes:

| Section | What's Included |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Scope of Work** | Detailed description of services to be performed |
| **Pricing** | Cost breakdown and payment schedule |
| **Timeline** | Expected completion dates |
| **Terms & Conditions** | Legal terms and policies |

---

## Receiving a Proposal

### Email Notification

When a proposal is ready, you'll receive an email containing:

- **Subject**: "Your Reserve Study Proposal is Ready"
- **Summary**: Brief overview of the proposal
- **Link**: Direct link to view the full proposal in your portal

### In-App Notification

You'll also see a notification in your portal:

- A **badge** on the notification bell icon
- A **pending action** card on your dashboard
- The study will show status: **"Proposal Sent"**

---

## Reviewing a Proposal

### Accessing the Proposal

1. **Click the link** in your email notification, or
2. **Log in** to your portal and go to **Reserve Studies**
3. Find the study with status **"Proposal Sent"**
4. Click **"View Proposal"** or **"Review Proposal"**

### Proposal Details

#### Scope of Work

Review what services are included:

- **Study Type** - Full study, update, or limited scope
- **On-Site Inspection** - Details about the site visit
- **Components Included** - List of assets to be analyzed
- **Report Deliverables** - What you'll receive (PDF report, executive summary, etc.)
- **Funding Analysis** - Types of funding scenarios included

> **Tip**: Ensure all components you requested are included. Contact the company if something is missing.

#### Pricing

Understand the costs:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Base Price** | Core reserve study service fee |
| **Additional Fees** | Travel, expedited service, extra components (if applicable) |
| **Total Cost** | Complete price for the study |
| **Tax** | Applicable taxes (if any) |

#### Payment Schedule

Proposals may offer different payment options:

| Schedule Type | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Full Payment** | 100% due upon invoice |
| **50/50** | 50% deposit, 50% on completion |
| **Thirds** | 33% deposit, 33% at site visit, 34% on completion |
| **Quarters** | 25% at each milestone |
| **Custom** | Special arrangement (details provided) |

#### Timeline

Typical milestones include:

1. **Proposal Acceptance** - You sign and return the proposal
2. **Financial Info Collection** - You provide financial data
3. **Site Visit** - Specialist inspects the property
4. **Draft Report** - Initial report for review (if included)
5. **Final Report** - Completed reserve study delivered

> **Note**: Timelines depend on your responsiveness and scheduling availability.

#### Terms and Conditions

Important terms to review:

- **Cancellation Policy** - Fees if you cancel after acceptance
- **Payment Terms** - When payments are due
- **Warranty/Guarantee** - What's covered if there are errors
- **Confidentiality** - How your data is protected
- **Liability Limitations** - Standard legal protections

## Accepting a Proposal

### The E-Signature Process

Proposals are accepted through electronic signature (e-sign):

1. **Review all sections** of the proposal carefully
2. Scroll to the **signature section** at the bottom
3. **Type your full name** in the signature field
4. **Enter your title** (e.g., "Board President", "Property Manager")
5. **Check the agreement box** confirming you've read the terms
6. **Click "Accept Proposal"** or **"Sign & Accept"**

## Rejecting a Proposal

If the proposal doesn't meet your needs:

### How to Decline

1. **Don't sign** the proposal
2. **Contact the company** to discuss your concerns
3. They may offer to **revise the proposal**
4. If no resolution, formally **decline the proposal**

### Common Reasons for Rejection

- Pricing is above budget
- Scope doesn't include needed components
- Timeline doesn't work
- Terms are unacceptable
- Decided to use a different company

### What Happens After Rejection

- The study status changes to **"Proposal Rejected"** or **"Cancelled"**
- You can request a new proposal with different terms
- The company may follow up to understand your needs

## Amendments

Sometimes the scope of work changes after acceptance (e.g., additional components discovered during site visit).

### What is an Amendment?

An amendment is a modification to the original proposal that may affect:

- **Scope** - Additional or removed components
- **Price** - Cost adjustments
- **Timeline** - Schedule changes

### Amendment Process

1. **Notification** - You're notified of scope changes
2. **Review** - Amendment details are provided
3. **Approval** - You accept or discuss the changes
4. **Continuation** - Work proceeds with updated terms

### Amendment Alerts

You'll see amendment notifications:

- On your **dashboard** as a pending action
- Via **email** notification
- In the **study details** page

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I negotiate the price?**
A: Contact the company before accepting. They may adjust pricing or scope to meet your budget.

**Q: What if I don't have authority to sign?**
A: Have your board approve the proposal first. Many boards pass a motion authorizing a specific person to sign on behalf of the HOA.

**Q: How long do I have to accept?**
A: Proposals typically remain valid for 30-60 days. Check the expiration date in the proposal.

**Q: Can I accept part of the proposal?**
A: No, proposals must be accepted in full. Contact the company if you need a modified scope.

**Q: What if the price changes after I accept?**
A: The accepted price is locked in unless there's a scope change requiring an amendment.
